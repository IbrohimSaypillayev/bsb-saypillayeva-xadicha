introduction

Samsa is a baked pastry filled with meat, vegetables,
or cheese, popular in Central Asia, the Middle East, and
South Asia. It is traditionally cooked in a
tandoor (clay oven), giving it a crispy crust.

history of samsa

Samsa originated along the Silk Road,
influenced by Persian and Turkic cuisines. It shares similarities
with the Indian samosa and Middle Eastern sambousek.

ingredients&preparation

Dough: Made from flour, water, salt, and sometimes butter. Filling: Includes minced meat (lamb/beef), onions, and spices; vegetarian versions use pumpkin, potatoes, or cheese. Cooking: Traditionally baked in a tandoor or modern ovens

cultural significance5.varities across regions

Samsa is a staple in Uzbek, Kazakh,
and Kyrgyz cuisines, often served at gatherings. It has regional variations, such as fried samosas in India and stuffed sambousek in the Middle East

Cultural Significance

Samsa remains a versatile, globally appreciated dish, evolving while keeping its traditional essence.
